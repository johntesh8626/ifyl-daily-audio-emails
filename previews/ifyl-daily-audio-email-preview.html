Going to sleep does not switch off your brain. It turns out sleep actually means our brain is going to work. Now this is according to research from the Johns Hopkins School of Medicine. When our body is resting, numerous systems work automatically to help our muscles, tendons, organs, and other body parts heal and recover from the activities of the day. And our brain jumps into overdrive and works to reset itself. In the study, mice that slept well did three times better at remembering how to navigate a maze they just learned, compared to those that were sleep deprived. It's because during sleep, the levels of proteins that recalibrate or reset the connections between brain cells jump by 250%. The researchers believe this is because the brain cells crucial for learning and memory can only store so much new information. After that, the information needs to be organized, needs to be stored away as memories. So when we wake up, our memory is improved and we have more room for new information and we need to be asleep again to store it properly. That's how your brain works during sleep.
